# Quadratic Equations

## General Form
$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ where $a \neq 0$

## Solution Strategy
1. **Identify Coefficients**: Extract $a$, $b$, and $c$ from the equation.
2. **Calculate Discriminant ($\Delta$)**: $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$
3. **Analyze Roots Based on $\Delta$**:
   - If $\Delta > 0$: Two distinct real roots.
   - If $\Delta = 0$: One repeated real root.
   - If $\Delta < 0$: Two complex conjugate roots.
4. **Apply Quadratic Formula**:
   $$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

## Common Mistakes
- **Sign Errors**: Forgetting the negative sign in $-b$ or the $\pm$ when calculating roots.
- **Discriminant Calculation**: Incorrectly squaring a negative $b$ (e.g., $(-3)^2$ is 9, not -9).
- **Simplification**: Failing to simplify the radical $\sqrt{\Delta}$ completely or improperly cancelling terms in the fraction.
