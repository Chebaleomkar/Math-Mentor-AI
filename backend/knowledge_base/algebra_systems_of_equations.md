# Systems of Linear Equations (Algebra)

## General Form (2 Variables)
$a_1x + b_1y = c_1$
$a_2x + b_2y = c_2$

## Solution Strategies

### 1. Substitution Method
1. Solve one equation for one variable (e.g., $x$ in terms of $y$).
2. Substitute this expression into the *other* equation.
3. Solve the resulting single-variable equation.
4. Substitute the found value back into the first expression to find the second variable.

### 2. Elimination Method
1. Multiply one or both equations by constants so that the coefficients of one variable are opposites (e.g., $2x$ and $-2x$).
2. Add the equations together to eliminate that variable.
3. Solve for the remaining variable.
4. Substitute back into either original equation to find the exact value of the other variable.

## Types of Solutions
- **Intersecting Lines**: One unique solution $(x, y)$. Ratio: $\frac{a_1}{a_2} \neq \frac{b_1}{b_2}$
- **Parallel Lines**: No solution (Inconsistent system). Ratio: $\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \neq \frac{c_1}{c_2}$
- **Coincident Lines**: Infinite solutions (Dependent system). Ratio: $\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2}$

## Common Mistakes
- **Sign Distribution**: Forgetting to distribute negative signs across all terms when substituting.
- **Adding Equations**: Forgetting to multiply/add the constant terms ($c_1, c_2$) on the right side of the equals sign.
